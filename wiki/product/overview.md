# How the product works

Jazz Master is a web app that helps guitarists get better at jazz — a structured practice companion, not a chord-chart reference. The core promise (VIS-001) is **zero-tension practice**: open the app, pick up the guitar, and play. The app has already decided today's session from your level, goals, and history, and guides you through it exercise by exercise. It targets intermediate guitarists learning the jazz language — beginners in jazz, not on the instrument.

## The practice flow

The intended end-to-end session, once the guided-practice slice lands (strategy/goals.md "Now" item 3):

1. **Dashboard** shows today's plan, generated by the adaptive planner from a local profile plus practice history (EPIC-011, EPIC-012).
2. **Guided lesson** runs the plan exercise by exercise — drills like "play this ii–V–I in Bb, now in Eb, now with drop 2 voicings" — rendered guitar-first: fretboards, grip diagrams, and (later) standard notation with tablature (EPIC-008, EPIC-009).
3. **Grading**: self-graded first; recording with in-browser pitch/timing scoring is the staged riskiest bet, gated on a feasibility spike (EPIC-010, VIS-001).
4. **History**: the session lands in practice history; the dashboard shows streaks, scores, keys mastered — momentum (EPIC-012).

Content exists to feed drills and lessons, not as browsable reference — "the app decides, you play" is the differentiator.

## Module pillars

Each pillar maps to an epic (VIS-001): foundation (EPIC-001), chord voicings (002), progression trainer (003), practice tools/metronome (004), repertoire (005), ear training (006), curriculum & lessons (008), notation & tabs (009), recording & scoring (010), adaptive planner (011), dashboard & history (012), plus the multi-app platform track (013). Ways-of-working (007) is process, not product. Non-goals for now: social features, native mobile, notation *editing*, accounts/backends, bundled copyrighted material.

## How it works under the hood

Everything runs client-side; there is no backend and no account (ADR-002 — "logging in" means a local profile). Domain logic — notes, intervals, chord spelling, scales, arpeggios, fretboard positions, with enharmonics correct by construction — lives in the pure `@jazz-master/theory` package; curriculum content (exercises and lessons in `apps/web/src/content/`) references theory constructs rather than hard-coding notes, so a lesson is "the Bb major scale in position II", not a note list; pages compose reusable components (Fretboard, ChordDiagram) which render what theory computes; all persistence goes through typed localStorage stores in `apps/web/src/storage/` (details: architecture/overview.md). The EPIC-013 platform track (Astro on Cloudflare Workers, ADR-006 when written) may change the shell; the local-first product model stands until an ADR says otherwise.

## Built today vs pending (as of 2026-07-06)

Done: app shell with routing and sidebar, the theory core, fretboard and chord-diagram components, and the persistence layer — EPIC-001 (foundation) is complete. EPIC-008 (curriculum & lessons) is complete: the exercise/lesson content model (TASK-011), the first lesson pack (TASK-012 — 10 scales/arpeggios lessons across 3 levels), and the guided practice runner (TASK-013) — from the Practice page a user starts a lesson, is guided exercise by exercise (fretboard positions, countdown, got-it/shaky/missed self-grades), and the session — completed or abandoned — persists as a `PracticeSession` record in the `sessions` store. EPIC-011 (adaptive planner) is complete: first-run onboarding or skip writes a local `PracticeProfile`, `/profile` edits it, and `/practice` shows a persisted Today's plan generated from profile, lesson metadata, session history, and date; each plan item has a reason, starts the runner, and ticks done from completed sessions. Everything user-facing beyond that — dashboard/history, drills, notation, recording — is backlog: the other practice modules are stub pages.
